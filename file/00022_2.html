triumphant. It does not Seem possible that such a thing could be permitted – and I feel as if I should never lose the horror of it –

I can’t write you a long letter tonight as I am leaving town early in the morning & have billions of letters to answer – also must write at length to My Boss – whom I love dearly –

[p. 4]

as he is in Paris now you will probably see him – I envy you both. I had such an interesting letter from my Mr. Read of the British Museum telling me all about their Egyptian trip. He says that Mr. M. has acquired some very good things. As I am now beginning to go off on visits of a week or more to my friends – will you mark your letters personal so I can have them forwarded to me – I have lots to tell you but alas no time – Your Belle
