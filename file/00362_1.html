Sunday Nov. 1 –

Dearest B.B. –

For once, the war has been entirely driven from my mind – and this because of your letter in which you say you will probably not come over – I can’t tell you how the news has affected me,

I feel somehow as if I were adrift in the ocean and I am overwhelmed &

[p. 2]

filled with a distaste for living. I suppose that sounds theatrical but it does not half express the lassitude and melancholy that has settled upon me since receipt of your letter. Without fully realizing it myself I had been counting the days and more or less just living along until you should come – I did not really care a bit about anything I was doing or anyone I was seeing it was all just a pour passer le temps until you came and
