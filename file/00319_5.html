Tuesday 11. p.m.

Adored one – as usual I am writing to you before jumping into my – our – bed. Had oceans of people at the Library all day & was thoroughly exhausted when I went home at five o'clock to give Gellet Burgess tea and hear about his approaching nuptials. At six o'clock Read came and we motored down

[p. 8]

to the Beau Séjour restaurant on Long Island for dinner. The country was simply glorious – we took the motor parkway which is a wonderful motor road of 45 miles built by a syndicate of men headed by Vanderbilt and Mr. Morgan – The apple trees were all in wonderful pink bloom and the fields a wonderful lavender blue of violets.
