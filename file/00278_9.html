Daarrling – Your letter of yesterday came as I was about to finish this. It was dear and sweet and “illuminating” – I was dreadfully afraid that you would think me gushing about the Jap – for that was so far from my feeling. He sent me [illegible] yesterday a most enchanting little golden

[p. 14]

lacquer box filled with loose single violets. Of course the box was nothing important, nor were the flowers – but the combination as a thought was delicious and I must admit that I was simply enchanted. He is coming to dinner tomorrow night and I have asked only one other person, my German prof. Arnold Genthe, as he lived in Japan for several years & speaks Japanese
