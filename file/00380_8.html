Swinburne said foul Coleridge, among facts “He is lonely and incomparable” –

Well I was a bit surprised to see the gent for he was certainly painted and powdered and his eyebrows made up – but he did play like an angel – He played, for the first time in America!!! a “berceuse heroique” that Debussy had written in hommage [sic] of King Albert of Belgium and the manuscript of which he had sent to Copeland – then he played some wonderful Spanish things and Debussy’s “Apres-midi d’un faune” which he (Copeland) had arranged for the piano.
