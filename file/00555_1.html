Friday evening

22 April, 1927

Dear B.B –

I am thrilled by just the appearance of your book, which reached me this week – and on Sunday, I am going to give most of the day to reading it –

Duveen has sent me some 10 copies (for which I shall of course pay him) to send to those dumb brutes the Trustees etc. of the Metropolitan Museum – as he says he “cannot very well” do so – Well I will – although I can’t believe there are enough brains between them all to understand and appreciate it – I will also send copies to Yale, Harvard & Princeton with a personal letter in each.

I have reached the point where I don’t care one single damn about that old Met. Mus. any more – I am infinitely more interested in the Fogg & some of the Western museums –

By the next mail I am sending
