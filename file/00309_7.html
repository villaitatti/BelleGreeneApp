At Worch yesterday I met Kent, who told me how he had begun buying Chinese things long before anyone else knew of them – He was madly in love with the two statuettes that Winthrop bought. I am so sorry that you did not get the lady – Later –

I have had great excitement today in placing the Kwan-yin I bought from Worch to my everlasting rumination. It was a heart rendering as well as joyous performance for I had to through [sic] out pretty nearly everything else in the

[p. 10]

place – The only things that are not utterly killed by the jade & the Kwan-yin are my Italian pictures – but I am greatly relieved and soothed by the emptiness of the rooms. did I tell you that Worch gave me the small marble head he wanted me to buy? He says that he showed you a photograph of it and that you thought it finer than his three large heads for which he is asking $12,000.
