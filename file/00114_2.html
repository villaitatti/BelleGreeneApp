I had hoped that dear B. B. would absent himself longer, so that you would have a bit of leisure in which to prepare the ancestral domain

[p. 4]

but I must say he seemed to be in a fever of impatience to return. I am sorry about the frescoes – Far be it from me to say “I told you so” but I was a bit dubious when I first saw the sketch but hoped the colour would bring things together. I am seriously distressed about B. B.’s health and am anxiously awaiting another letter from him with better news. I am sure the masseuse will do him lots of good –
