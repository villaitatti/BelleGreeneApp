We have received four paintings at the Library of which I will write you fully in my next when I will know more about them – I am glad to say the Ghirlandaio has come over also – I do not know how you feel about it but I adore it & always have –

Duveen arrived a day or so ago & says he has some fine things for us – I hope so –

Do you know Fairfax Murray & his collection of original drawings? They have been offered to us – Just at present I beg you to keep this to yourself – until I write you that we have decided about them one way or the other.

I have finally abandoned my Southern trip. I do not doubt that I will have to go to England some time this winter on several matters which have arisen but I suppose there is small hope of my seeing you –
