Tuesday 10 May, 1921

Dear B.B –

For the past four or five days, I have been occupied solely with laziness and reading – not even a glimpse of the Library until the morning.

On Thursday I went to the Livingstone’s in Bar Harbour – They

have no particular qualities – merely very nice, very well bred quiet old New Yorkers who lead “that” kind of a life – I did little but sleep – drive, go out in a seaplane & water boat and read, “L’ombre de la Croix” of the Tharauds, and various ‘essays’ from Roger Fry’s Vision and Design.
