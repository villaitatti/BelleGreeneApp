On the other hand, he had nothing good to say for them in any way –

Read asked me if he might not go with Murray & me to the Winthrop's so I said certainly – and we advanced upon 37 Street about five o'clock – Pretty frail little Miss Emily was at the tea pot rather drunk and shy in the presence of the great – Winthrop immediately began piloting us through the rooms and as I suspected and feared, not five minutes passed before your name was mentioned. This was in reference to some

[p. 11]

horrible portrait of a man by an English artist & he asked Murray to look at it, saying "Of course Berenson scorns it and will have nothing to do with it, but I think it will interest you " which was break no. 1 – Then he showed us the Rossettis & Burne-Jones which he had bought from Murray & I praised them to the skies. His Chinese things except for one head did not interest me much and as a whole I did not like his art collection. Some
