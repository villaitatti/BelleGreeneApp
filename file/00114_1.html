Tuesday November 29th.

Dear “Miss Mary” –

I was enchanted to receive your letter even with its bad news of B. B.; now that he is back in your safe keeping I am less worried about him than I have been.

In the same mail as yours, I received a letter from him, mostly about the house which please me greatly – I will quote you what he

[p. 2]

says “Home at last and everything but the frescoes in the library a thousand times better than I expected. The house is simply ravishing, the pictures framed & beautifully hung, the carpets down & every thing in place with about it all a certain air of sanctuary purity and distinction – All that is as I like it.
