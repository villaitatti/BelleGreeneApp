[p. 9]

talking to me about Claude Phillips the other day – apropos of an article he had written about Mr. Morgan’s paintings and he said that he was of undoubted learning & the only thing he knew against him was that he was a creature of Agnew’s –

I am knee deep in all sorts of affairs at present & must stop – I am going to Newport on the 5th to stay until the 9th and from there to the Berkshires for a few days – then back here forever

I will try to write you by next Wednesday’s boat –

By the way – I am trusting that you will understand that it is because I really like you that I wrote you as frankly as I did in my last – wanted you to know the attitude of these people toward you – just as I would trust you to inform me in a similar case – & you must never fail to tell me when people are unfriendly to me of course I know I must have [change in pen] scores of enemies but you can understand that I am the last one to hear of them. Personally I am always rather glad to hear of them for it keeps my head its normal size

Don’t fail to write me as often as possible B.G.
