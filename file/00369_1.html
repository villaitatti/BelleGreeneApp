December 29, 1914

Dearest B.B. –

I have been in bed for over a week with a paralyzing combination of tonsillitis and laryngitis – a raging fever, a buzzing head and a throat swollen to the bursting point – and in such real pain that it was necessary to give me opiates almost every night as otherwise I should not have had a wink of sleep. Today is my first day on and I found such great joy two letters from you awaiting me at the Library – Many thanks dear, for your cable which reached me on Christmas Day – I was very unhappy and depressed the whole day and wanted to do nothing but creep, head and all under
