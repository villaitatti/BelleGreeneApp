He brought me over a copy of that dreadful thing he published “Blast” I daresay you saw it – And he has taken over Gertrude Stein's and considers her “Three lives” one of the finest things in modern literature – He also brought me a dreadful thing by Jules Romains called “The death of a nobody.” I dipped into it for a few minutes and was affected by its ghastly nature, a sort of dead-in-

[p. 14]

lifeness, the husk and peculiar odour of a corpse.

It is dedicated to Roger Fry – Nuff sed –

In the evening some friends of mine invited Murray and myself to the opening of Ed Knoblauch’s play “My lady’s dress” which I understand had quite a run in London – It is really a series of little one act plays, strung together under guise of a dream –

Some of them were exceedingly clever – One or two deadly dull.
