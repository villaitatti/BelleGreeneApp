April 20 – 1915

Dearest B.B. –

When your letter came telling of your illness I was sitting up in my library room at home for the first time in ten days after a devastating attack of grippe – I was so miserable if you have ever had the beastly disease that I could do nothing but lie

[p. 2]

in bed and groan – I am, at this moment, as weak as a kitten, but I am sure the enforced rest will have good results later on – I was taken ill two days after I came back from Boston and that is why I have not been able to do anything about the photographs of Loo’s two paintings of which I wrote you – I am however going to the Library this afternoon
