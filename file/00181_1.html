Feb. 1, 1912

Dear B.B. –

I received your letter of Jan. 21 this morning. It is really dreadful that you should be so worried about me and it really makes me feel wretched. I seem to have been pretty damned selfish and I’m awfully ashamed of it. I’m not

[p. 2]

going to tell you any more how much I like you and how much you mean in my life until I see You, for I can understand from your letter just received as I really never have before that my protestations and my slipshod, careless and evidently heartless method of correspondence don’t seem to “gel” – therefore oh daarrling [sic] if I don’t tell you much about my feelings toward
