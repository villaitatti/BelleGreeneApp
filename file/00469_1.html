Friday 4:30 p.m.

[In blue pen: March 11, 1921]

Dear B.B. –

Our telephone conversation yesterday was so unsatisfactory that I want to send you this line immediately upon my arrival here – mainly to explain that my [^last^] letter was not intended as a horror – Your wire did not intimate that you

[p. 2]

found it so and then over the telephone you insisted that it was "horrid" – I am sorry because my idea in writing was to clear away the debris instead of piling more on –

You see, my dear, [^daily^] letters are dangerous weapons or instruments unless the correspondents are au fond in harmony. They are so easily misread and the spirit misconstrued.

Mine was a call to arms that we might have peace –
