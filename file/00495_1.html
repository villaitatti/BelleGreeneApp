15 July, 1921

Dear B.B. –

I am afraid that I sent you a very cross letter yesterday – Here’s where I try to make amends – as it is delightfully cool after a heavy thunderstorm and I am feeling quite fit – Last night Barry Faulkner and I had dinner together and then went on down

[p. 2]

to a dance at the Gardiner [sic] Hale’s – I spent most of my time sitting on the grass in their back yard (they call it the garden!) and drinking endless glasses of – water, pure and unstrengthened while most of the others (about 30) proceeded to get more or less pie-eyed.
