May 15, 1911

My dear – My daarrling B. B. –

I have just received your letter telling me of the death of Mary’s mother and truly and sincerely I am quite sick about her. I know how much she loved her mother and how that love was returned and I fear there will be a void in her life that nothing can fill.

[p. 2]

I wrote to her today – but very timidly & very humbly. I mean so little to her & it is such a big loss that she has to bear that it seems well nigh impertinence for me to write at all.
