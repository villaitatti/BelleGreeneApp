Thursday March 25

Dearest B. B –

You may well be cross with me for not writing the more so as I await your letters with such ridiculous impatience – gobble them up and read them half a dozen times when they do come – Before Mr. Jack sailed I had not a single free second – you may think this absurd but it is true. He put so many things into my hands now that I don’t dare stop to think of

all there is to do – I have been at the Library almost every night continuously for a month – I expected to drop into bed for a week’s sleep the minute he sailed – but lo and behold solely because of his unparalleled stupidity assininity [sic]and childish temper he left without settling up with Duveen for the 18th century things and I had to conclude the matter after he sailed we closed it on Tuesday for 2 million dollars.
