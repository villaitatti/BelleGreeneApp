Monday 12:30

Dear B. B. –

I got in from the country at 11:30 and found your letters of Friday & Sunday. I am lunching with the Harcourts at one so cannot write much now – I dare say you are wise in your decision not to come here

[p. 2]

but it confesses to a weakness in us both which is not much to our credit & rather annoys me – I don’t know, at this minute what to say to you about the things you bought me in Paris – of course you were a dear to do it, but you ought not to have & it rather complicates things. I rather fear
