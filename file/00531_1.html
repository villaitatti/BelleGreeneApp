April 28th, 1926.

Mr. Bernard Berenson

I Tatti

Settignano

Florence, Italy .

Dear B.B.

Just a line to say that I am having photogrphs [sic] of odds and ends of the della Rovere Pontifical, taken for you to-morrow, as on the next day, we are shipping it with about half a dozen others to Paris, for the exhibition of Italian books and manuscripts, to be helf [sic] there during May and June; but I know that you have received more photographs than you have noted in your letter, because I had them especially taken for you the last time you were here. They are undoubtedly mixed up with some other photographs of your collection, but I will send you duplicates.

I am going to have photographs taken of the unusual (for me) rare Italian manuscripts before 1350 or 1400 hundred at the Paris Show. Do you want any of these?

Do you remember two Italian histrionic initials of which I sent you photographs several weeks ago, our manuscript number 558 c.d. One represents the Trinity in human form, and the other, The Baptism in the Jordon [sic]. You referred me to Toesca page 528 which is O.K. but just after sending it to you, I found in a photograph of some manuscript offered, a representation of Pope Gregory, giving Communtion [sic], which is undoubtedly by the same artist, the faces, and various characteristics correspond with our two miniatures, but the interesting thing is, that beneath the miniature is an inscription reading: [ handwritten: (see over )]

perhaps you may be able to check this up in some way.

We are hoping to acquire five really A.l. manuscripts (before twelve hundred, [ handwritten, underneath: 1200]) if we succeed, I will send you photographs.

Ever devotedly yours,

[ signed: Belle da Costa Greene]

Belle De Costa Greene.

Director.
