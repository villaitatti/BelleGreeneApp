a youth from Boston came to my house for tea (his name was Steele) and the Trio were there – Steele had some messages for me from Denman Ross and proceeded to deliver them amidst grunts and groans from Murray who loathes, despises and abominates Ross – finally to change the dreadful subject I began to tell Steele of the really delightful portraits that Murray had made of my friend Nancy Furst – when the irresistible

and unrepressible [sic] Steele breaks in with “Have you seen Ross’ painting? You know Berenson says he is one of the three greatest painters of the last century” – that was the last straw – Murray got up on his bandy legs – took his hat and as he went to the door, threw over his shoulder “Why drag in the other two?”

Murray was so disagreeable that I just let him go
