Tuesday April 28th

9:30 p.m –

Dearest B.B. –

I came over to the Library tonight to write some letters as I had been so bothered by people all day, that I had not been able to do anything; and an unexpected joy was to find your letter of the 16th on my desk – quite wonderful for Italy, only 12 days – I cannot really understand your antagonism to Italy or rather the Italians, nor can I understand how any one

[p. 2]

could have lived among them as long as you have and still be so little of them, but of course, being You, you must be right about them – I should love to know Placci, all that you have ever written about him [^makes him^] seems fascinating – Don’t run away with an idea that I was utterly unappreciative of Ross – I got many a glimmer and would have gotten-got many many more had he shut up occasionally and you must remember that his gush over his extremely poor Persian illuminations was quite a shock to me.
