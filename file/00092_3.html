I enclose d two wires that awaited me here – In reply I wrote Seligmann that I was sorry not to have seen him as there were several things I wanted to talk over with him and which I hesitated to write about – but that as I understood Rey was coming to New York within a week I would see him & asked him to communicate my ideas to J. S. & that it would then be for him to decide whether he desired my friendship or

[p. 5]

my opposition. of course I couched it all very discreetly but he’s no fool – and he knows of J.P.’s affectionfor me & trust in me & I think he’ll take the tip –Nousverrons– I am not going down to luncheon – will finish this in time to send off at Cherbourg – B. G.

5 p.m –

I am so distressed that I have not been able to write another line. I discovered some other friends on board & I have not been alone for a second & the Purser says I must give this to him immediately if I want it to go as there is no mail boy & they are sending it
