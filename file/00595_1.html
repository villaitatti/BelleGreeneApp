April 17, 1947

Mr. W.M. Ivins, Jr.

Woodbury, Connecticut

Dear Mr. Wal-Dung:

Why could you not choose a more aromatic alias?

In re the enclosed and your last communication, also your lecture – What have you done in the last 30,000 years? There is quite a bit I could say about the enclosed but I will exercise my usual and well-known ladylike restraint and only say – why not take someone your own size to kick downstairs? You can interpret “size” physically, intellectually, complimentarily or otherwise, as you wish.

Why not let sleeping lions lay? When disturbed in their slumber they are apt to bite and chew.

However, like B.B. (as you say) this outburst is “covered full of love and affection and all the amenities of the season” including the smallpox –

Sincerely yours,

(Sgd.) Belle da Costa Greene
