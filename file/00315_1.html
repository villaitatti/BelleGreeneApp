Saturday May 2

Dear B.B. –

I have just received, read, and partly digested your letter of the 19–21 April; which I find very interesting but a trifle gray and weary, that is, it gives me a feeling that you are not as joyously bouyantly [sic] happy as you were in Paris, but I may be misinterpreting the spirit of it. In regard to Ethel

[p. 2]

Harrison's letter, I think both you and she are disgusting – She, in herself, and you to persist in keeping up relations with her. What a charming idea (from her letter) you must have given her of my Entourage, of which by the way, you know little – Your remarks as to Read and Murray I pass over as I have heard them before – however I will faithfully report their actions and conduct to you.
