Saturday, April 12

Dearest B. B. –

I think you will know how almost impossible it is for me to write – but you are all I have left now

He came back to me and the Library he loved so much, last night at six o’clock. We fixed his room for him almost exactly as it was on Christmas

[p. 2]

Day – all red and white roses and a few wreaths. I cannot tell you how beautiful it looks – It has resulted in just what we aimed at – a home coming.

As for me I feel as one enveloped in a dense fog – I can neither think nor see into the future. Not even you, knew all that he was to me – besides every other relation he was a son and a father confessor.
