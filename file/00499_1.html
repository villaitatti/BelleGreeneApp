February 6th. 1922.

[handwritten: Dear B.B. –]

Mr. Mather has just sent me your most generous check of $250.00 as a contribution to the great Harvard-Princeton journal.

I can’t tell you how [underlined in pen: splendid] we all think it is of you to come so immediately and spontaneously to our assistance, but, inasmuch as we are going to ask so very much of you in the matter of articles and advice, I am overjoyed to be able to tell you that we need not impose upon you for financial assistance as well.

We had not had a meeting of the Board (doesn’t it sound Grand?) for a long time, and so the members had not been made aware by me, that our initial financial difficulties had already been solved for us, by the extraordinarily kind responses to such appeals as had been made by us as a group.

Of course, as the Journal develops, or as we hope it will develop, we may have to make further appeals, but as you mean help to us that no amount of money could buy, we shall not trouble [underlined in pen: you] in that way.

It has been decided to name [underlined in pen: The Che-ild], “Art Studies-Mediaeval, Renaissance and Modern” and to publish but one number the first year.
