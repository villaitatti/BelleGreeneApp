Thursday Sept. 4, 1913

Dear B. B. –

You and your Mrs. Wharton seem terribly far away! – (Hope you notice the jealous note!) – I am up to my ears still in work – but every once in a while I stop and remember that you are actually coming over this winter. I can hardly believe it and am really excited.

I wonder if you

[p. 2]

are planning to go to Boston the same day you land – also, I wonder if you would like to go (both of you) there in my car or if you prefer the train.

The trip is a rather long one in the car unless you stop over somewhere for the night, but the train I think is horrid and this last terrible wreck onthe New Haven may have made you nervous –

There is, of course, plenty of time to let me know
