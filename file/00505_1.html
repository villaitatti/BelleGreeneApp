12 Feb. 1924

Dear B.B. –

I am today sending you the photographs of the Italian MS. which Offner ran down to the neighborhood of his friend Pacino– the attribution (he does notinsist upon Pacino) himself, is to me, most convincing judging from the photographs Offner has shown me. I have promised him that he might use the photographs in a further "Pacino" publication which he plans. I have only seen

him once since, as it is such an effort for me to be unaffectedly civil to him–I don't know why particularly, as I am inclined to admire his scholarship, and he has never been any thing but unctuously polite in return for the occasional "slaps" I cannot resist giving him. Of course my annoyance is obviously because I have no real reason for not welcoming him with open arms.
