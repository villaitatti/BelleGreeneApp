Saltaire Sept. 26. 1919

Dearest B.B.

For the first time in years I feel somewhat rested. Somewhat at peace – and lots like writing to you – I came down to this little Island two days ago to stay with my sister Ethel (Mrs. Oakley) – Do you remember her I wonder? The "blondish" one?

I slept all the first day and night, in bed on a sleeping porch under heaps of blankets, for of course the place is as flat as your hand and the wind just tears across it –

Practically all day yesterday I slept on the beach, rolled up in blankets like a papoose. And today I feel beautifully cleared up inside and out –
