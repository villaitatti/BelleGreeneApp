Wednesday. Mar. 30 –

I have just received yours of the 19th – This is not a reply, merely a hurried scrawl on the matter of the Yerkes catalogue.

I am so distressed that you did not speak to me about it earlier, for I am able to send you only the first part at this writing. The second part, containing the rugs, tapestries etc. & for the

the [sic] really important part of the catalogue, was entirely subscribed for immediately upon its announcement – the edition was limited to 150 copies at $45 per – but the demand was easily three times the supply, and I fear it will be impossible to recover one although I have put Kirby on the track.
