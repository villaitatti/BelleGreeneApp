it was just like those lowdown Jews to put such a ridiculous interpretation on it all – but I could see he was much disturbed –

He was to sail on Wednesday (this was Monday) but he fully intended to return in the Spring ! He immediately sat himself down and wrote to Mr. Jack, disparaging the Duveens and saying that he thought that they all should have a meeting together, Jack, the Duveens, and himself.

[p. 10]

And for this he would be very glad to cancel his passage & stay over – In any case he would like to have an interview with Mr. Jack before he sailed.

Mr. J. telephoned of that there was no necessity of a further-interview & he wished Mr. Murray a “bon voyage” – I was so pleased, and Murray was furious –
