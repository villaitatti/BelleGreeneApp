Sunday Oct. 4, 1914

Dearest B.B.

I have spent the day in bed, with a well defined, but perfectly proper pain beneath my belt – And reading newspapers (which consumed the whole morning) and Da n Pan-Germanism which I have not yet finished.

Our papers are full of

protests from Germans of our anti-German attitude, it rather annoys me that the papers give them so much space – The fighting seems to be very stubborn with no gains on either side and I am (like everyone else) almost suffocated with gloom –

Also nothing outside of my work interests me but reading of the War either in the papers or one ofthe 1000 books that
