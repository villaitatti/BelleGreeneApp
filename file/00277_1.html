Tuesday Mar. 3, 1914

B.B. my Cleverest and Oneness.

I have, this morning, devoured your train letter. It has made me so happy and so “big” feeling that I shall be kind to all the world today – It makes such a difference to me when you write a

[p. 2]

fairly longish letter because then I have time to become absorbed in it and you – I have beenwildlydistressed about you and Mary, as all the trains due to leave either N.Y. or Washington after yours, were inconceivably detained.
