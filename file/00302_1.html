Tuesday April 7th

B.B. Belovedest –

Much as I adore you, this daily epistle is getting on my nerves!! So there!!! –

There is little to chronicle today as I have been pretty busy with my correspondence – this morning bright and early Joe Duveen appeared on the threshold – He had presented himself numerous times before but I had been too busy to see him. He wanted to talk about the collection and see how much

[p. 2]

of it he could buy – desperately anxious most of all to know if anything was to be sold, as he wanted to regulate his spring buying in accordance. I told him as a grand great secret that something most assuredly would be sold and then he plunged into speech and told me all the things he must have – needless to say, I was a “glad” listener. I took careful note and told him I would see Mr. Morgan about it etc.etc. and perhaps let him hear something before he sailed. All this for your private ear alone darling – then dear old Father Hyvernat came along, with plans for buying more Coptic leaves. I put my foot down on that and kept it down until he left.
