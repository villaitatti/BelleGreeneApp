Noone [sic] but the Carnegie or Rockefeller foundations would endow it for you, I fear – and you naturally could not expect it.

Failing the establishment of I Tatti, I still think the Fogg Museum, in every way the best and the most suitable resting place for the collections – I am certain that if they were properly approached they would build you a special wing – of half the building if necessary, and there, as you know, their one idea would be to add to it and maintain it, properly and creditably.

I cannot think of any other place where your work would be so adequately appreciated and be made so available and so widely known.
