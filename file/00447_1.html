Tuesday, April 1

Dear B.B.

This must be a scrawly pencil affair as I have been laid up here all day with a threat of the "flu" – You can imagine my rage at having all my plans & engagements upset this way – I hope to be sufficiently well by tomorrow noon to get off of to the Bensons until next Tuesday, where thank God, Mrs. B. will take care of me – I sent word that I thought I ought not to come, but she insisted that I could be twice as well taken care up there as here – That of course

is perfectly true but it does seem pretty bad to foist an invalid on her when she has a houseful of guests – I really do love her – Mrs Morgan prefers him, but I think that may be because Mrs. B. rather puts it over a bit on Mrs. M. –

I am so damned sick of this sneezing & wheezing – I caught the germs while in Cambridge on Monday & Tuesday – The damned inn was so damp and I foolishly and out of sheer personal vanity did not wear my woolies – Always a foolish omission in England I find –

I did have a splendid time there – Dear old Sydney Cockerell just shut up shop
