Saturday, June 13, 1914

Dearest B. B. –

Only time for a line this morning as I am off to luncheon and the Palo games with Eugene Meyer and some friends of his – Last night we went to a truly remarkable moving picture show for which I believe d’Annunzio was responsible. It was called

[p. 2]

Cabiria and the events took place at Rome and Carthage during the 3rd century B. C. – The advertisement said that the mere staging had cost $250000 – but I should think it must have cost more for they had the most vivid scenes of the African desert, etc. The story in itself was thrilling but the mounting was really the most superb thing I have ever seen – I was thrilled through and through
