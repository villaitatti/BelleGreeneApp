Wednesday Aug. 12

Dearest B.B. –

Our shipping is so entangled that one cannot tell when a ship is going out – I expected the New York to sail this morning & so mailed a letter to you yesterday, now I learn that the stupid old ship is not going until tomorrow – We are kept in a state of excited confusion by the inaccuracy of our news– One day the two German cruisers are reported captured

[p. 2]

by the English – the next they are reported somewhere about Genoa! One day we are told the Germans are repulsed with terrible losses at Liège and the next that they have taken the ports. We can put no reliance in anything as except direct reports from the British Admiralty – and they are few and far between.
