He’s conceited enough to imagine that I am all ready – I gave that weekend with joy & hurrahs to Agoness & will punish Read by making him go up there alone – He’ll be blackly furious but I don’t care – I see by the papers today that Lady Johnstone is on board with him, so he will doubtless forget my very existence until he lands – He sent me, by the way, a perfectly enchanting Han pottery camel, with two noble humps and of a

[p. 11]

deep dull clay colour. I like it immensely and only hope it is not worth much as I could not accept anything expensive from him but I doubt if it cost him much or anything. I’m just [double underlined: waiting] angel till he lays them keen eyes of his on my jade – Won’t he take a breath? He’ll probably say it cannot be right! Will you return the enclosed letter?
