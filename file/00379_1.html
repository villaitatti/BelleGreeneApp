Friday, Jan. 29, 1915

[ written in upper margin :P.S. I’m bored to death with Agnes and don’t see her at all any more and don’t intend to ever again so your prophecy came true you’ll see – she’s a dreadful weight! B.]

B.B. Beloved –

All this fiendish nerve-racking week I have been trying to write you, but not one moment have I had – It has happened that I have been out every night – not because I wanted to be on the go, but because of the things I was asked for , which I happened to want to see or hear. The thing I have done this week which will doubtless

[p. 2]

interest you most is a visit I paid to Frick’s house you remember it was not quite finished when you were here and is on 5th Avenue facing the park – the site of the old Lenox Library – He came down to the library for mehimself and also brought me back after luncheon which I thought a kindly stunt. One whole wing is devoted to the picture gallery, which contains some things I had not seen there before,
