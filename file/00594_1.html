Gideon Putnam Hotel

Saratoga Springs, N.Y.

July 29, 1946

M. Bernard Berenson

I Tatti

Settignano

Florence, Italy

Dear B.B.:

I have been shockingly bad about writing you, and you are, as always, an angel to show your forgiveness by your letter of June 2nd just received – one month in transit!

To answer your queries first – The Swarzenski book is the only publication we have made in these horrible war years. I, too, think it a very good piece of work. It is a marvellous manuscript.

Meyer Schapiro of Columbia is working on our earlier 10th Century Beatus Ms. and if he ever finishes his study we hope to publish it, but he has been “at it” almost a year now. We also have several other publications in view.

Frankfurter of the Art News tells me that he will “be seeing you” shortly, and I have given him hectic salutes to you. As you doubtless know, he has the most interesting and worth while idea of devoting a complete issue of the Art News to the Gardiner collection and hopes that you will write an introduction. I do also, but cannot see you writing a long spiel about yourself, and of course, it is all due to you.

I am trying to recuperate, meaning get somewhat, if only a little bit, revitalized after a bad two or three years. I will doubtless be back “on the job” before you receive this.

Louis Levy has been here but I did not get to see him as the M.D. kept me abed for a week or two. I would have been interested to hear his Duveen you et al talk, as I understand from a lawyer friend of mine here, he is writing a book on that and other of his causes celébres.

When you have time, write me of all you are doing, whom you see, etc., etc. I understand that there are several of our “vagrants” over there and that Offner is in the offing – or perhaps he has arrived?

My Love to you and best regards to Nicky. How is she?

Devotedly,

[ handwritten: Belle (d)]
