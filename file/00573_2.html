Morgan Manuscript 397

Aesop and other fables in Greek, containing

1. Kalilah and Dimnah – fragments of Greek translation

2. The Physiologus

3. Vita Aesopi

4. Fables of Aesop

5. Fables of Babrius

6. Asteia of Hierocles

Treatment.

1. General description of the manuscript including discussion of the script, miniatures and dating.

2. Brief description of the contents, and identification of this manuscript with Cryptoferratensis.

3. Detailed account of each part.

Importance of this Manuscript

Kalilah and Dinmah – has unique features, this part alone deserves publication.

Physiologus – Has complete passages which are defective in all other Greek Mss.

Vita Aesopi – related to papyrus fragments, particularly to the Oxyrhynchus papyrus which could be very well restored on the basis of Ms. 397.

Babrius, Fables – the known Mss. are at Mt. Athos and the Vatican, with a few scattered single fables. Ms. 397 has 31 fables, a larger number than in other Mss. and includes 4 not elsewhere known.
