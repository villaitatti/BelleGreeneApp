April 21, 1913

Dear B. B.

Your letter of April 8th received today (only!) was very dear and very comforting to me – It is fine to know that you are there and that you are staunch – that helps a lot –

I feel as if the ground itself had been taken from under my feet and it is impossible for me to find any interest in

[p. 2]

life just as present – you see you (naturally) did not know how dear he was to me – how even in my fits of anger and rebellion he showed his affection for me – I feel that I shall always regret the times I chafed to leave him and go to Europe.
