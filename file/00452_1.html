1 May, 1920 –

Dear B.B –

I wonder if this much heralded day is as calm, sunnyand uneventful in Florence as here – It is really too ridiculous – I expected fireworks at least and hoped for a bomb or two –

In reality it has been one of the most beautiful and peaceful days that I have spent here – a youthful friend got a motor through the English Embassy and took me to Tivoli – It was

nearly divine – almost as wonderful as our last walk at Settignano – but I must tell you a secret – I actively missed you !

God alone knows when I can leave for Paris – Some people here think that the threatened French strike will last for a week at least, just long enough to prevent my catching the Adriatic. The Holy Fathers think it playfulness only – and Cooks (as usual) know nothing –
