October 9th, 1919.

Dear B.B.,

Because of a discussion, in which your name has been pretty freely used, I want to ask your free and frank opinion (for myself â€“ not for publication) of Offner's article on our Macrino d'Alba, in the last number of Art in America. To me it is the most filthy exhibition of meaningless word-mouthing that I have come across for a long time.

I could say much more, but will refrain. I am told you regard his work highly, and shall not rest peacefully until I know if this is true, and, if so, why am I so fearfully wrong?

I think Gertrude Stein's portrait of Mabel Dodge is a master-piece compared with this "drool".

Always Yours,

[ handwritten : Belle]

Sig. Bernard Berenson,

I Tatti, Settignano,

Florence, Italy.

[ handwritten: over]
