Wednesday March 18 11a.m.

Dear B.B. –

I did not know that any post could be worse than the Italian but evidently Bryn Mawr excels there as in everything else. I am so relieved that you are settled for tea on Saturday afternoon – and with so harmless a person! Unless I hear from you to the contrary I shall meet you at Knoedlers at 3 p.m. sharp. I shall probably be there myself a few minutes earlier. To be [^have you^] detained in the Deanery would be against the White Slave Act and I should have the authorities proceed at once – we are filled up with a New Haven meeting here now – c’est à rire – the meeting is being held at the direction of the men, who have supposedly resigned from all control or interest.
