If Robinson were the Kaiser’s own son, he could not be more German in spirit – Why dear B.B.? Can you tell me?

He says that the dealers are all bluffing in order to keep up prices – but as the German army will not be conquered or subdued in one year or two, that they will soon have to acknowledge that “art” has no value at present – He says that the Duveen’s are throwing the biggest bluff of all – He spoke very warmly of

[p. 10]

your friend Miss Lou Sands, who had personally brought him Valentiner’s letter. He urged me to come up and see a wonderful collection of Chinese paintings which had recently been given to the museum, but says that they will not buy anything this year – He told me with unrestrained joy, that Mr. Jack, could not sell anything from the museum for two years at least – I naturally did not say anything.
