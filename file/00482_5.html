27 April 1921

Today I lunched at the Round Table at the Museum as Breck’s guest We had a delightful hour marred only by the clumsy Teutonic attempts at wit of Bosch-Reitz – He is an amiable, thick headed thing! Lythgow [sic] was most interesting about Winlock’s new find in Egypt – a painted tomb – You will probably see it when you go there – Do you remember my speaking to you of an over-life size Roman (B.C.) terra cotta figure? The Museum has at length bought it, but please don’t mention the fact, as, for some crazy reason, they are intent upon keeping it a great secret for some time – I am the only outsider who knows (at least, so they said) and
