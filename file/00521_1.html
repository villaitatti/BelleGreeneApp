28 Aug. 1925

Dear B.B –

I received your letter the morning I left Paris. It evidently crossed the one I wrote you –

I could not get even a berth on the Olympic or Mauretaniaand so had to take this old tub – which has almost driven me mad by its slothful progress – it reminds me of a dead whale

[p. 2]

wallowing upon the top of the water, first on its back, then on its side, then on its belly.

I am so anxious to get back that the enforced rest (8 days it's to be!) of this swine among boats, has had the effect of nearly driving me mad –

I am rather despairingly afraid for Jack Morgan –

There has rarely, if ever, been such love and devotion and complete living as existed between Mrs. Morgan and himself &
