Monday afternoon

April 13

B.B. you angelic one –

It was perfectly thrilling to find your steamer letter awaiting me at the Library this morning and to know that hereafter I shall have them uninterruptedly – Poor dear, what a terrible voyage you had – I never heard of such a combination of ills – So now you know how devilish and enthralling a cold can be! And that horrible medicine that was to prevent seasickness You

[p. 2]

remember I told you that Glaenzer would not bother you – what I can tell you now, but could not before is that, in reality he firmly believes that you are trying to “get in” with Seligmann! in fact that you are to be Seligmann’sfor the asking!
