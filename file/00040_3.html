I was sorry and disappointed to find that we already had similar or better impressions of all the Rembrandt etchings in your friend’s collection. You see he had only 32 and while they were exceptionally fine

[p. 6]

they were the usual pieces which are part of the nucleus of every collection. I shall be glad to speak of the lot to other collectors here if you or he wish – but it will doubtless fetch a better price in Paris or Berlin –

We are in the midst of preparations for the Hudson-Fulton week to which I look forward with horror. I am declining all I can but some things I have to attend. I loathe parades & pageants & any sort of a thing which suggests a crowd & the thought of a week of such is nerve-racking to me – Aurevoir [sic] – B.G.
