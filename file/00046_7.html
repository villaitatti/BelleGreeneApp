I expected we would be – I had a letter from Mr. Read who is crowing over what he calls Bode’s discomfiture but several of the men over here are very strong in Bode’s defence. Of course Laffan made a lot of it in the Sun but he was the only paper for some days to notice it – Langton Douglas seems to be a very slave of Bode. He lauds him on every occasion & proclaims him “the

[p. 8]

only true God” in defiant accents. I enclose a bit from the Evening Post about the matter – I wish I had time to tell you of all the interesting people who have been here & of the interesting things I have done – but I must stop this moment. Justi of Berlin is coming tomorrow. Yesterday I entertained the Society of Printers 52 strong – It was awful – I feel like a human island – I am glad you have at last a competent Secretary It must be a great relief to you – The last letter I got from you (Nov. 7) was very short & snippy – Brute that you are – Yours Belle
