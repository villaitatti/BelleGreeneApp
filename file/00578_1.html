29 October, 1934.

Dear B.B. –

Ever so many thanks for your letter. Of course we were aware of the Deësis pattern, but as you can see from the other descriptions, we avoided ‘form’ titles for several reasons – chiefly ‘going English’.

I [ underlined in pen : very ] much wish that you would give me your other corrections and queries as that is more helpful and useful to me than any amount of ‘kindly phrases’.

We have had the panel showing the [ apostrophes added in pen : ‘Legend of the Loreto House’] cleaned off, and I am sending you a photograph to see if you still think it by Saturnino de'Gatti. Will you let me know?

There were many other really important manuscripts which I wanted to put in our show in the N.Y. Public Library, but as it had been confined (with the exception of our Purple Gospels) to a [ underlined in pen : picture ] show, I could not do so.

The Walters Gallery is being formally opened next Friday evening. I am going down tomorrow to stay with Alice Garrett for the rest of the week and all the frivolities and formalities connected with the opening. You would be pleased to see it, I am sure, after our clearing-out process. We left, on an average, one object out of ten or twenty, but in every case that one is a beauty!
