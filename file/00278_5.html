Ralph Sanger – Lagrange came to the Library about twelve o’clock, and Troubetzkoy boomed in about 12:30 to ask me for luncheon. He knew Lagrange quite well & so asked us both, so then I telephone Mrs. Sanger to join us, which she did and went over to the little Italian restaurant.

A number of people we all knew were there, in-

[p. 8]

cluding Paul T. and his wife, Strafagnaigla, an actorine named Dorothy Donnelly – Constance Richardson, my German master, Arnold Genthe, Pinchot & Carl Van Vechten. We all sat at a long table and had a fairly amusing time. Paul T. has done a very wonderful statuette of Lady Constance – the best thing I have ever seen of his – Mrs. Sanger started
