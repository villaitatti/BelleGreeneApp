Sunday 27 July 1924

Dear B.B.–

Here is your "yellow peril"!

I have been so wickedly – shamelessly bad about correspondence for the last 5 months having neglected every correspondent I have in the world, due to a combination of hectic times at the Library – hectic times of my own – and a large amount of selfishness & self indulgence.

Also I am always overcome by the hopelessness of writing to people so far away – The letter is cold and dead and valueless by the time it reaches you and by the time a reply reaches me, I have almost forgotten what it's all about!

This week end, is the first one I have not spent with, or rather on, the Rich, but yesterday I came up here to be with Bobbie, in a tiny bungalow right on – almost in – the Sound, near Stamford, Conn, which I took for him for the summer.
