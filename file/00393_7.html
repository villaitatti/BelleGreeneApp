I do not know how true it is, but I dare say they will buy them if they can – They have used every argument, in vain, to persuade Mr. Morgan to sell his pictures. Frick offered Mr. Morgan $500,000 – for two of his English pictures – that I would not give ten cents for personally – Frick has made a will in which he has left his house and collection on

[p. 11]

5 Ave to the City. I think that the dispersal of the Morgan collection made him realize that it had better be done in his lifetime – The Duveens (mainly Joe) seem to have set their faces agin me and are loudly proclaiming the fact that it is I who put Mr. Jack up to asking such high prices for his things – they virtually told him to his face that he knew nothing of the values and, as they put it, "it's that Miss Greene" etc etc.
