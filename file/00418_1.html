Monday, p.m.

Dearest B. B. –

I am writing this in the greatest rush to tell you that I am almost distracted with disappointment by your wire – You can understand now why I could not make all plans for Paris as I had expected. I am now straining

every resource as to the return journey from there. I can get over with great delay & difficulty – but Sir Montague Barlow, an M.P. who went over on official business last month told me he had to wait ten days for proper papers – boat etc. & as you know the circumstances of my coming here you can understand that I cannot wait to return
