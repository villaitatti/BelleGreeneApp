We reached home at about seven thirty – Raphael was off to a dinner given him by the Japanese Club so Read & I dined together at Delmonicos – then he came back home with me & we chinned until one o’clock – He told me, and I tell you (very confidentially) how disgusting Lady Johnston was on the voyage over on the Olympic. He said she told the filthiest stories he had ever heard. They were all

[p. 16]

together all of the time as Lady J. had been put in Murray’s care by Mrs. Guthrie He also said that Lady J. was almost forcibly ejected from the Saloon as she insisted upon smoking there. Read loathes her and thinks she is mostly responsible for the fact that her husband has not advanced to a more important post.
