Islip – Long Island

Monday afternoon

Julyne 21 – 1909

Sweetheart –

In the midst of a giddy week-end party – the hour I snatch to write to you is an oasis in a desert of hysterical activity & show

This is such a funny party – I am staying with the Wagstaffs and they have filled their really adorable house with

[p. 2]

an appalling mixture of literary, artistic & musical lights & a smattering of the “smart set” whom I loathe – Sometimes I find it amusing – for instance last night life long feuds were made at the dinner table over the Baconian theory – of all the futile discussions I ever heard that was the worst one – now Richard Watson Gilder & the others [sic] literary lights are not speaking – Julia Marlowe is sulking because she heard us ripping her Juliet to
