Thursday morning – April 16

Dearest – How [ double underlined : too] adorable to have your first letter from Paris today so really you in tone and spirit – I cannot bear for you to have that Tang painting without my seeing it – will you be a darling and have it photographed? I ache with envy that I was not with you to see the things at Bing and Vignier – I can feeling you thrilling now in that coma-like quiet of yours – the painting must have been

[p. 2]

maddeningly extravagant as you did not divulge the price but I am so glad that you have it – And your account of Seligmann, as you will know, interests me mightily and thank you so much for giving it to me in detail. We are going to return the book cover, but I shall wait until Read & Murray get here and so give it back with their ultimatum, although their opinions are by no meansfinaleven to me, we shall (or rather I already have) arrange to have it backed up by Durlacher & Murraymarks
