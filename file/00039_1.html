August 31 – 1909 –

This is but a poor “Signal” to you. I have had two shockingly busy days with a week of the same stretching before me; and I seize this minute as I do not know when I shall find another in which to write you. Miss Thurston (my one assistant) and Mr. King – Mr. Morgan’s Secretary are both away on vacations and I am beset on all sides – I doubt if there will be [double-underlined: any] [signed: Belle Greene] when they return.

Thank you for the Durrieu letter which I return. As I wrote you I shall not go further in the matter at present. If I go over this winter I shall probably look them up. I
