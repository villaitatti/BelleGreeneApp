Friday June 19th

5 p.m.

Such a horrid dreary day darlint – I had a delicious evening at Greenwich last night and was loved and feted to my heart’s content – Mr. Truesdale & some other men who were there talked long over the business and political situation. They all seemed to think that now Roosevelt was out of the running, that Whitman stood an excellent chance for the Governorship

[p. 2]

Now that S.R. had resigned – All the men were strong for him and said they would vote for him – I was really distressed to hear what small opinion of (and admiration for) they had of Jack Morgan – they say that his course in regard to the art collection, will not only hurt him very much in the business world but will lower him in the estimation of everyone –
