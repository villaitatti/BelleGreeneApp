Friday 6. p.m –

I had hoped to find a letter from you awaiting me – but no luck – Jack’s excitement was that the newspaper had got wind of the Chinese Porcelain sale and he was in a blue funk as they have not been appraised by the state yet and then would be an awful legal row on if it became known. I told him there was nothing to do but for him to refuse to see reporters, keep absolutely quiet and make Duveen (who had started the report) deny it – I called Chuck Henry up and swore we’d break the contract
