March 10, 1928

Mr Bernard Berenson

i Tatti Settignano

Florence

Dear B.B

I am [underlined in pen: really] very sorry that your letter of January the thirtieth has not been answered before this, but I have been completely deluged by Library matters, and all the bothersome details connected with the Supplementary Library building which Mr Morgan is erecting adjoining this one; and have besides, been in such beastly health that whenever possible I go to bed directly upon reaching home.

The reason I did not send you a copy of the Roxburghe publication earlier, is that there were so very few of them left (we decided not to sell any) that I had to see Mr Morgan about the distribution of most of the six remaining copies. I find [inserted in pen: ,] as of course I expected, that he is delighted to send you a copy, without any question of whether it is a final Harvard disposition of[edited in pen:r] not; we will ship it in a few days, and trust that it will reach you safely.

I am enjoying one of my annual colds at the moment, and just as soon as I feel less stuffy, I will sit down and write you a long personal letter.

Ever devotedly yours,

[Crossed out in pen: Belle da Costa Greene] [written in pen: Belle]

BG:VB

P.S. Do you mind sending a line of acknowledgement [underlined in pen: to Mr Morgan] when the book reaches you?
