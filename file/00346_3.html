Thursday, Aug. 3

Well dear – the d—d – old ship is again postponed until tomorrow so I have time for another word –

We had an enormous rail-road meeting here yesterday, about 30 new Directors, Presidents etc. of all the big lines – I think it is in re the Rate Regulation decision – Mr. Jack bossed the whole job just as his Father used to do – I left here about

[p. 5]

7 in the midst of the most dreadful thunderstorms I have ever witnessed – I was frightened almost to death & reached home to find my Mother an hysterical heap on the bed. We none of us could touch any dinner & I proposed to lie down about eight thirty but I was so nervous I could not keep any part of my body still so as the storm had passed, I suggested to Mother that we go out for a walk – We did and afterwards stopped in at a moving-picture
